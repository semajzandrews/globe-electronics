# Globe Electronics — SIGNATURE

**Concept: "The Bench."** Globe is a 15-year neighborhood repair-and-sell shop on Main St
(est. 2009). They open devices up and bring them back: phones, tablets, TVs, stereos,
Bluetooth speakers, Blu-ray players. The brand truth is the workbench and the human hand
holding a tool over an open board. Handcraft mood, locked.

## Lane (locked, honored)
- Accent: `#C75300` burnt-orange (the "powered-on / revived" glow, the solder heat).
- Display font: Erode (Fontshare) — a warm, slightly imperfect humanist serif; reads handcraft, not cold-tech.
- Body: Archivo / system humanist sans for legibility.
- Layout: sticky pinned scroll-story (the repair walkthrough is the spine).
- Materiality: warm graphite bench + copper/solder + amber bench-lamp light. NOT the
  blue-gradient electronics-store cliche.

## Signature moment — "The Repair, step by step" (sticky pinned scroll-story)
A `position: sticky` stage pins a real device-repair board to the viewport while four
numbered chapters advance on scroll: **Diagnose -> Open -> Fix -> Revive.** As you reach
the final step, the burnt-orange "power" line traces across the board and the device
"comes back to life" (warm glow blooms, the screen lights). One confident scroll-coupled
moment, photography-led, that literally encodes the pitch ("we bring it back").
- Driven by scroll progress (CSS sticky + a scroll-linked progress var), reduced-motion
  collapses to the finished/revived frame. No WebGL dependency, so it cannot blank-hero.
- Distinct from past signatures: not a hover-reveal (Ramos), not a drawn line (sisters/pulse),
  not an ambient loop (empire), not a one-shot elevation (plhome). It is a PINNED, photographic,
  scroll-scrubbed teardown-to-revive sequence.

## Distance / Novelty / Escalation
- DISTANCE vs siblings: warm burnt-orange + Erode serif + sticky teardown-story is a unique triple.
- NOVELTY: electronics/phone stores default to blue gradients, carrier-store grids, neon. A warm
  craftsman's-bench editorial with a scroll-scrubbed repair sequence is industry-rare.
- ESCALATION: a genuinely scroll-pinned multi-chapter photographic sequence with a live
  "revive" recolor is a harder scroll mechanic than a single on-view draw.
